ABOUT

Sri Lanka's tea industry dates back to the 19th century and is one of the largest and most important industries in the country, employing over a million people and contributing significantly to the economy. The tea is grown in the central highlands and is renowned for its unique flavor and aroma. Sri Lanka produces black and green tea, with black tea dominating production. Despite challenges such as unpredictable weather and declining prices, the industry remains a vital part of the country's economy and culturalheritage. It promotes Sri Lanka as a top producer of high-quality tea and maintains a significant role in the global tea market.

Story of Ceylon Tea

James Taylor arrived in Ceylon in 1852, settling down at the Loolecondera Estate, Galaha. He begins a 19-acre tea plantation on the Loolecondera Estate in 1867, laying the foundation for what would become Sri Lankas largest export industry for over a century. In 1872, a fully equipped tea factory began operating on the Loolecondera Estate. In 1873, Ceylon Tea made its international debut when twenty-three pounds of tea produced by James Taylor reached London.

Regions Of Tea Cultivation

Ceylon tea and the tea produced in each Sri Lankan region have unique characteristics resulting from differing climates and elevations. Kandy is famous for mid-grown teas with a variety of strengths and styles, depending on elevation. Lower elevation produces large, strong-flavored leaves, while higher elevation produces smaller, mild and delicate leaves. Uva region tea has a distinctive flavor used mainly in blends, and is known as low-grown tea grown below 2000ft. The region's soil and low elevation result in rapid growth of tea bushes and beautiful, long leaves ideal for rolling.

Health Benefits Of Tea

Ceylon tea is globally renowned for its unique taste and qualities, and is highly regarded in the international market. Sri Lanka's weather conditions and rich history contribute to its greatness. Ceylon Tea comes in several types, including Black, Green, and White. Ceylon Black Tea has a strong flavor with citrus overtones and higher caffeine content. Ceylon Green Tea has a distinct, strong, and leafy flavor. Ceylon White Tea has a sweet flavor with overtones of honey and is light in color, also known as 'Silver Tips.' Ceylon Tea also offers numerous health benefits.
